description: "Calendar and Microsoft 365, and how Mothy drafts messages you confirm rather than sending on its own."
---

Mothy can read your calendar and, when your organisation turns it on, connect to
your Microsoft 365 to read your tenant calendar and email and draft messages and
events for you.

## Connecting your account

If the Microsoft 365 connector is enabled for your organisation, you connect your
own account once. After that, Mothy can answer questions about your calendar and
email. Your connection is stored securely and can be removed at any time, and
every use is recorded as a summary, never the contents.

## Actions are confirmed, never automatic

Mothy never sends anything on its own. If you ask it to send an email or create a
calendar event, it prepares a draft and shows it to you; nothing goes out until
you confirm it, and an unconfirmed draft expires on its own. This keeps a person
in control of every outbound action.

## The privacy position

The connector is the one door through which Mothy reaches outside your appliance,
it is off until your organisation turns it on, and when on it reaches only your own
Microsoft 365 tenant. See [Security and privacy](/mothy-docs/security-and-hardening/).
