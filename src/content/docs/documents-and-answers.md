Mothy's core job is to answer questions from your organisation's documents.

## Getting documents in

Your administrator loads your organisation's documents into Mothy: policies,
handbooks, guidance, forms, and so on. Mothy reads PDF, Word, plain text, and
markdown, keeping headings, lists, and tables intact so the structure is not lost.
If a document has important tables, such as a fee schedule, a Word or markdown
version reads more reliably than a PDF.

## How answers stay grounded

When you ask a question, Mothy finds the passages most relevant to it and answers
only from them. It combines meaning-based search, which finds passages that say
the same thing in different words, with exact keyword search, which anchors on
specific terms and figures. It quotes figures exactly, gives both the parts and
the total for a structured answer, and says plainly when something is not in the
documents. It does not invent an answer.

## Trusting an answer

Because answers come from your own documents, you can trace them back to the
source. Answer quality is measured against a fixed set of test questions,
including a check that Mothy refuses to give a figure the documents do not
contain.
